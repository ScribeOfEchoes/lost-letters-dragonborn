High Hrothgar – Final Reflection

I have worn many titles in my time—Arch-Mage of Winterhold, Harbinger of the Companions, Guildmaster of the Thieves, Listener of the Night Mother, Dragonborn of legend.

Each was granted not through ceremony or triumph, but through silence—rooms emptied by time, halls hollowed by loss. In truth, I did not rise to power so much as I endured longer than those who might have led better.

The College stood quiet when I arrived, its name carried more by memory than merit. Winterhold itself was half-buried beneath the sea, and its people spoke of magic as one might speak of fire—dangerous and best left untouched. The Companions had strength but little direction, weighed down by a legacy they no longer understood. The Guild scrabbled for coin beneath Riften’s rot, its roots severed. The Brotherhood had forgotten its own rites. The Blades? A ruin, more stone than blood.

Yet in each, I found cause to remain. Not out of ambition, nor pride, but duty—perhaps guilt.

I am no hero. The dragons are gone, but the land remains fractured. The war lingers. The gods are distant. Skyrim stands, yes, but it wavers. I could not heal it. I could only keep watch.

Some would call that failure. But if there is value in tending a fire that others abandoned, then perhaps this, too, is a kind of service.

I did not come to restore glory. I came because someone had to stand where others fell.

—Ysmir, Dragon of the North
