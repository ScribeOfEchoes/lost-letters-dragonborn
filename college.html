College of Winterhold – To the One Who Studies Alone

The College was already fading when I arrived. No lectures. No legacy. Just a shell clinging to a cliffside and a whisper of power held behind locked doors.

And yet, you remained. Practicing, reading, failing, trying again. Magic is a dangerous thing. But so is purpose. You had both. That made you more a mage than most Arch-Mages I ever met.

The world will always fear what it does not understand. You do not owe it your obedience—but perhaps you owe it your patience.

Hold fast. The sea does not stop for one voice. But it remembers the stone that held through the tide.

—The Arch-Mage
